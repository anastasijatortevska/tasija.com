About

I'm an MS student in computer science at ETH Zürich, working in theory. The question I keep circling back to: when does combinatorial structure make a hard problem tractable — and when it doesn't, how well can we still do?

Before ETH I studied CS and math at Columbia, where I spent two years TA'ing Discrete Mathematics and CS Theory for 300-student classes, and worked a few years as a software engineer. I grew up on math olympiads — IMO, EGMO (bronze), Balkan, Mediterranean.

Off duty I'm into art, video games, and martial arts. I hold a 1st dan in Goju-ryu karate and 2nd kyu in Shotokan karate :)

Research Interests

Discrete algorithms and complexity, broadly:

  • Approximation algorithms
  • Graph algorithms & graph theory
  • Computational complexity
  • Parameterized complexity
  • Combinatorics
